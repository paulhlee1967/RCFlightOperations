Renewals & Dues

How to record membership payments, handle special cases, and configure your club's dues structure.

About Payments

Before anything else, it's worth being clear about what this section of the app does and doesn't do.

RC Flight Operations does not process payments. There is no connection to credit cards, PayPal, Venmo, bank accounts, or any payment service — no money moves through this app, and members cannot pay dues through it. What you are doing here is recording dues payments your club has already collected through your normal channels (cash at a meeting, a check in the mail, a bank transfer, etc.). Once the money is in hand, you log it here so your member records stay current.

Your club decides how to collect dues — that's entirely up to you. RC Flight Operations just keeps track of the fact that you did.

How It Works

RC Flight Operations separates the member record from payment history. A member exists in the system year-round; a payment ties them to a specific calendar year. When you record a renewal, RC Flight Operations:

  1. Creates a payment record (amount, date, year, fees).
  2. Updates the member's renewal year to the paid year.
  3. Clears the "badge printed" flag so you're reminded to print a new card.

A member is considered Active when their renewal year matches the current calendar year.

Process Signup / Renewal

  1. Open the member's record from the Members list.
  2. Click Process Signup / Renewal (button near the top of the page).
  3. Select the year you're renewing for using the year buttons (previous year, current year, or next year are shown).
  4. Review Step 1 — Review Member Info. Any warnings (missing AMA, suspended status, etc.) appear here with links to fix them.
  5. In Step 2 — Record Signup / Renewal, choose the renewal type:
    • New / Late Renewal — full dues + initiation fee (use for new members and late renewals)
    • On-Time Renewal — regular annual dues, no extra fees
    • New Member (Prorated) — dues prorated based on join month
    • Complementary — check the "Complementary" box for zero-cost renewals
  6. Confirm and click Record Signup / Renewal.
  7. Step 3 — Print & Mail Checklist appears: print the badge, prepare a mailing packet, and mark each task as done.
AMA required before renewal You cannot record a renewal if the member has no AMA number on file, or if their AMA expiration is before December 31 of the renewal year. Fix it on the Compliance tab first.

New Member Signup

When someone joins mid-year, use the New / Late Renewal type to charge full dues plus the initiation fee, or New Member (Prorated) if your club offers a reduced rate for members joining in the second half of the year. RC Flight Operations calculates the prorated amount based on:

  • The member's date joined (set on the Membership tab)
  • The prorate rules configured for their membership type

An initiation fee can be included — configure it under Administration → Configuration → Dues Rules.

Late Renewals

Use New / Late Renewal when a member renews after your club's normal renewal window. This type includes both the annual dues and the initiation fee as a late charge. You can adjust the displayed amount before confirming if your club handles late fees differently.

Prorated Dues

Prorated dues are calculated per membership type. In Administration → Configuration → Dues Rules, each membership type has:

  • Annual dues — the full-year renewal amount
  • Prorated dues — the reduced amount for members joining later in the year
  • Prorate start/end month — the window during which the prorated rate applies

Complementary & Free Members

Two ways to handle members who don't pay dues:

MethodWhen to useHow to set
Complementary renewal One-time complimentary year (e.g., club officer courtesy) Check the "Complementary" box when recording the renewal — records $0
Free membership flag Permanent free membership (e.g., honorary member) Check "Free membership" on the Membership tab; a renewal still needs to be recorded each year

Life Members

Check Life member on the Membership tab. Life members still need a renewal recorded each year (as complementary) to show as "Active" — but the system will remind you. The Waived / Free Memberships report shows all life and free members.

Configuring Dues Rates

Go to Administration → Configuration → Dues Rules (Admin role required). Each membership type (Adult, Youth, Senior, Spouse — or whatever labels your club uses) has its own row:

SettingWhat it does
Annual duesFull-year renewal amount in dollars
Prorated duesReduced amount for members joining later in the year
Initiation feeOne-time fee for new members and late renewals
Prorate start monthFirst month of the year when the prorated rate applies (e.g., July = 7)
Prorate end monthLast month when prorated rate applies
Changes to dues rates apply to future renewals only — they don't retroactively change existing payment records.

Post-Renewal Checklist

After recording a renewal, the app shows a fulfillment checklist in Step 3. Here's what each item means:

  • Print ID Card — opens the badge print view in a new tab; mark as done once printed
  • Print Mailing Packet — opens the letter and envelope print view; mark as done once sent

If the member has unchecked “Allow postal mail” on their Contact tab, the envelope and letter screens show a postal opt-out notice. The address still appears so you can send legally required mail or make a one-off exception; confirm with the member before routine postal mailings.

Checking off items records the date and who completed each task. The dashboard's "Badges unprinted" count resets to zero for a member once their card has been printed and marked done.
