Members

Everything about adding, editing, and managing your club's member records.

Member List

Go to Members in the top nav. You'll see a paginated list of all members with quick-glance info: name, membership type, renewal year, AMA status, and badge print status.

From the list you can:

  • Click any member's name to open their full record
  • Click Renew to go straight to the renewal workflow
  • Click Print card to print their ID badge
  • Use the Export CSV dropdown to download the list

Search & Filter

The filter bar above the list lets you narrow members by:

  • Status chips: All, Current, Inactive (plus membership type when viewing current members)
  • Membership type: Adult, Youth, Senior, Spouse
  • Search box: Searches first name, last name, and email in real time (press Enter or click Search)
Filters are additive — you can combine status + type + search to get exactly the list you need. The URL updates as you filter, so you can bookmark a particular view.

New Member Wizard

Adding a member is a guided five-step wizard that walks officers through contact details, compliance, membership type, first payment, and printing/mailing — without jumping between separate screens. A progress stepper at the top shows where you are in the flow.

StepScreenWhat you do
1 — ContactNew member wizardName, email, phone, mailing address, emergency contact, and photo.
2 — ComplianceNew member wizardAMA number and expiration (use Verify AMA membership), FAA if applicable.
3 — MembershipNew member wizardMembership type and date joined.
4 — Record signupProcess Signup / RenewalChoose New Member (Prorated) or New / Late Renewal, confirm dues, and record the first payment.
5 — Print & mailProcess Signup / RenewalPrint the ID card, prepare a mailing packet, and mark fulfillment tasks complete.

Steps 1–3 use Next / Back within the wizard form. After step 3, click Save & record signup — the app saves the member and continues automatically to step 4.

Resume an in-progress signup If you save contact/compliance details but leave before recording payment, open the member from the list and click Continue signup, or use Process Signup / Renewal. From the renewal screen, warning links take you back to the right wizard step to fix missing AMA info or other issues, then return with Save & return to signup.

Adding a Member

  1. Click New member (top right of the Members page).
  2. Complete wizard steps 1–3 (contact, compliance, membership).
  3. Click Save & record signup to open step 4 and record the first payment.
  4. Finish step 5 — print the badge and complete the mailing checklist.
Classic edit form for existing members The tabbed Full edit form is for members who are already in the database. New members always start in the wizard; New member no longer opens a blank edit form. Use Full edit form on an existing record when you need every tab at once (payment history, notes, status flags).

Payment History Tab

A read-only log of every payment recorded for this member. Columns: date paid, membership year, dues amount, initiation fee, late fee, and whether it was a complementary (comp) payment.

To add a payment, use the Renew workflow rather than editing this tab directly. See Renewals & Dues.

Status Flags Explained

A member's status is shown as a badge in the list and on their record. Here's what each one means:

Status shownWhat it means
Current Renewal year is the current calendar year, payment is on file (or life/free membership), and not marked inactive or suspended
Inactive Everyone who is not a current member — including expired renewals and members marked inactive on the Membership tab
Suspended Marked suspended on the Membership tab — renewals cannot be recorded until this is cleared
Life Club life member — shown in addition to current/inactive status
Free Free or complimentary membership — shown in addition to other status

Member Photo

Upload a headshot on the Contact tab. Supported formats: JPEG, PNG, GIF (max 5 MB). Photos are stored under uploads/member_photos/ and used for badges and internal display.

When you approve a website application that includes a badge photo, the app imports that image automatically so you can print the badge without a separate upload.

The photo appears on the badge front when your club's badge template includes a photo element. It does not appear anywhere else in the app.

Deleting a Member

Deletion is permanent Deleting a member removes all their data — contact info, payments, and photos. Consider marking them Inactive instead to preserve history.

To delete: open the member's record, scroll to the bottom, and click Delete member. You'll be asked to confirm. Administrator and Membership Manager roles only.
