AMA & FAA Compliance

Track AMA membership and FAA registration for every member — with live lookup to verify AMA numbers without leaving the app.

Overview

AMA-chartered clubs are required to verify that members hold current AMA membership before they fly. RC Flight Operations makes this straightforward:

  • Store each member's AMA number and expiration date
  • Verify AMA membership live without leaving the app
  • Block renewals for members whose AMA has expired
  • Run a compliance report to see who's expiring soon or already expired
  • Optionally track FAA TRUST/Part 107 registration numbers and expiry

Compliance fields live on the Compliance tab of each member's edit page (Members → [member name] → Edit → Compliance).

AMA Numbers

Enter the member's AMA membership number in the AMA number field. This is the number on their AMA card — typically 5–7 digits.

The AMA expiration date is the date their current AMA membership expires. AMA memberships run calendar year (January 1 – December 31), so most members will have an expiration of December 31 of the current or next year.

AMA life member? Check the AMA life member checkbox. This suppresses expiration warnings for that member — no expiration date is needed.

AMA Live Lookup

On the Compliance tab, click Verify AMA membership. RC Flight Operations queries the AMA member lookup API and returns:

  • Member name on file with AMA
  • Current membership status (active / expired)
  • Expiration date — which is automatically filled into the AMA expiration field
  1. Enter the AMA number in the field.
  2. Click Verify AMA membership.
  3. Review the returned info — name should match your member.
  4. Click Save member to persist the expiration date.
Name mismatch? If the AMA lookup returns a name that doesn't match your member, double-check the number. Don't record a renewal until you've confirmed it's the correct AMA number.

AMA Status Colors

A colored indicator appears next to the AMA expiration field:

ColorMeaning
Green AMA expiration is more than 60 days away — all good
Yellow AMA expiration within 60 days — remind the member to renew AMA
Red AMA has expired — member cannot fly; renewal will be blocked

FAA Registration Numbers

Two types of FAA registrations your members may have:

TypeWho needs itExpiry
TRUST certificate Recreational flyers flying under 49 CFR Part 44809 (community-based) Does not expire
Part 107 Remote Pilot Certificate Members flying commercially or outside recreational rules Expires every 24 months — track this in the FAA expiration field

RC Flight Operations stores one FAA number and expiration per member. If a member has both a TRUST number and a Part 107 license, store whichever is more relevant for your club and note the other in the member's Notes field.

Compliance Report

Go to Reports → AMA/FAA Compliance. This report shows members split into sections:

  • AMA expired — members whose AMA expiration has already passed
  • AMA expiring soon — expiring within the next 60 days
  • FAA expired — members with an FAA expiration in the past
  • FAA expiring soon — FAA expiring within 60 days

Each row links directly to the member's Compliance tab for quick updating. The report can be exported to CSV or emailed to your club's compliance officer.

How AMA Blocks Renewals

When you try to record a renewal, RC Flight Operations checks two things:

  1. Does the member have an AMA number on file? (Or are they an AMA life member?)
  2. Is the AMA expiration date on or after December 31 of the renewal year?

If either check fails, the renewal is blocked and you'll see a warning with a link to the Compliance tab. Fix the AMA info, save, then come back to record the renewal.

This check exists because AMA requires clubs to verify current membership before allowing members to fly at sanctioned fields. Recording renewals for members with expired AMA would put your club's charter at risk.
