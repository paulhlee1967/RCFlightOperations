Incident log

Use Incidents in the top navigation to record safety or field events for your club. Each incident belongs to your club only.

Overview

The incident log helps boards and safety officers keep a dated record of what happened, where it occurred, and how it was handled. Common uses include near-misses, equipment or airframe damage, injuries, property issues, and notes for AMA or insurance follow-up.

Who can use it

  • Administrator and Membership Manager — open the list, add new incidents, and edit existing records.
  • Club Staff and Report Viewer — open the list and review incidents read-only (no add/edit).

If you do not see Incidents in the nav, your role may not include it. Ask an admin to review your account in Users & roles.

Add or edit

From Incidents, use Add incident (or open an existing row). Fill in the incident date, location, type, severity, and status. You can optionally link a member when a specific person is involved. Use the description and Action taken fields for what the club did in response.

Consistency helps later Using the same location wording (e.g. “Main field — north pit”) and picking severity carefully makes year-end summaries and board reviews easier.

Search & filters

The list supports filters for type, severity, status, and calendar year, plus a text search across description, location, and linked member name. Use pagination at the bottom for long histories.

AMA reporting

When your organization files with the AMA or needs an internal paper trail, use the AMA reported and AMA reference fields on the incident form. These are optional; your club defines its own policy for when to check them.

AMA membership and expiration tracking for individuals is handled on each member record and in AMA & FAA Compliance — the incident log is separate from those compliance reports.
